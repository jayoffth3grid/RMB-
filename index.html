My dearest,

I keep starting this letter over. Every version sounds too small for what I mean, so I'm giving up on getting it right and just going to tell you the truth instead: you make ordinary days feel worth writing down.

It's the small things, mostly. The way you save me the last bite without being asked. The way a hard week gets lighter the moment I hear your key in the door. I used to think love announced itself — it doesn't. It just quietly rearranges your whole life until you can't remember the shape of it before.

I think about the version of me who hadn't met you yet, and I feel a little sorry for him — he had no idea what he was waiting for. He was lonelier than he let on. He kept the porch light on for something he couldn't name.

Now I know. It was this. It was you, sitting across from me, laughing at something not even that funny, making the whole room warmer just by being in it.

So here's the truth I keep almost-writing and finally will: I choose you. Not just on the easy days, but on the loud, tired, in-between ones too. Today, tomorrow, and every unremarkable Tuesday after that.

Forever yours.

The seal can be broken as many times as you'd like.
